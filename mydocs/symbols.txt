Key symbols in Alice in Wonderland carry deeper meanings:

Rabbit hole: Represents the gateway to the unconscious mind and the transition from the ordinary world to the realm of imagination and dreams.

Playing cards: Symbolize the artificial nature of social hierarchy and how easily established orders can be shuffled or overturned.

Tea party: Represents social conventions and the sometimes meaningless rituals of polite society.

Mirror and reflections: Symbolize self-examination and the search for identity, especially prominent in the sequel "Through the Looking-Glass."

Size changes: Represent the instability of identity during childhood and adolescence, and the relative nature of power and perspective.

Croquet game: Symbolizes the arbitrary and unfair nature of some social systems and competitions.
